DEFINITIONS

Forebrain:
The anterior part of the brain, including the cerebral hemispheres, the thalamus, and the hypothalamus.

Occipital:
Relating to or situated in the back of the head

Temporal Lobe:
Each of the paired lobes of the brain lying beneath the temples, including areas concerned with the understanding of speech.

Gyrus:
A ridge or fold between two clefts on the cerebral surface in the brain.

Sulcus:
A groove or furrow, especially one on the surface of the brain.

Hippocampus:
The elongated ridges on the floor of each lateral ventricle of the brain, thought to be the centre of emotion, memory, and the autonomic nervous system.
